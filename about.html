About Breezy Risk Advisors

Founded by Joerg Proeve

What Breezy Risk Advisors Is

Breezy Risk Advisors is an independent risk advisory firm. I don't place policies. I audit them. My incentive is to tell you what is actually in your coverage.

What I Do

I start with your policies, your contracts, and your operations. The deliverable is a Risk Intelligence Report: a structured, line-by-line analysis of your insurance policies against your operations, contracts, and regulatory requirements. For community banks and credit unions, that means reading your cyber policy, fidelity bond, and D&O coverage together and mapping how they interact against real incident scenarios. For managed service providers, that means comparing your Tech E&O and cyber coverage against the contracts you sign with clients and the services you deliver.

The output is a report with specific findings, policy-language citations, and a prioritized list of what needs to change. Not a summary. Not a second opinion over coffee. A documented audit you can hand to your board, your examiner, or your broker.

Who I Work With

Breezy serves community banks, credit unions, and managed service providers across the United States. The insurance review is conducted remotely and is not limited by geography.

Community banks and credit unions. I work with institutions where three overlapping policies (cyber, fidelity bond, D&O) create coverage confusion that only surfaces at claim time or during an examination. More on community bank insurance →

Managed service providers. Small to midsized MSPs in growth mode, adding service lines, closing enterprise contracts, or serving regulated verticals. I review Tech E&O and cyber coverage against the contracts and services that create real liability.

How I Am Different

I am the independent layer that verifies your broker's work. Not a replacement.

Your broker places insurance and earns a commission on what they sell. That is a useful service, but it's not an independent assessment. I serve a similar function as an external auditor: independent, fee-based, no broker of record letter. I read the policy language, test it against real scenarios, and report what I find. An advisor who also sells you the product has a structural conflict. I do not.

What makes my analysis different from a standard broker review:

  • I read the full policy wording in detail, not just the declarations page
  • I test coverage against specific incident scenarios (e.g., wire fraud, ransomware, regulatory investigation, board liability)
  • For banks, I map how three policies (cyber liability, fidelity bond, D&O) interact and where each carrier points to the other two
  • For managed service providers, I compare cyber liability and Tech E&O coverage against client contracts and service delivery
  • I identify exclusions, sublimits, and warranty conditions that would realistically block a claim

How My Engagement Works

I work on a fixed-fee, project basis. No commitment to move your insurance.

You send me your policies. I review them. You receive a report and a walkthrough call. If you want to act on the findings, you take them to your broker, or I can help you find one. The audit stands on its own regardless of what you do next.

Who Refers Clients to Me

Many of my clients come through trusted partners who already have a seat at the table:

  • IT audit and compliance firms. SOC 2 auditors and cybersecurity consultants who assess your controls but do not review whether your insurance matches those controls. I close that gap.
  • CPA firms. Accounting firms that serve community banks and see insurance questions surface during audits and examinations.
  • Attorneys. Technology attorneys who review contracts and see indemnification clauses that exceed their client's coverage.

If you are an audit, compliance, or legal professional and your clients ask you insurance questions you cannot answer, let's talk.
