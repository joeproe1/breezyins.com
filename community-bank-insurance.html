The Three-Way Collision

Every community bank and credit union manages cyber risk across three separate teams that almost never sit in the same room. Your broker handles coverage. Your IT team or CISO handles controls. Your compliance officer handles examinations. The broker does not know what controls IT actually runs. IT does not know what the policy requires. Compliance may not realize that a failed examination can trigger or void a claim.

Coverage

Broker and Carrier

Renewal cycle. Policy language. Sublimits, exclusions, warranty conditions.

Controls

IT or CISO

Daily operations. Technical safeguards. MFA, EDR, patching, backups.

Compliance

Compliance and Risk Officer

Examination cycle. Regulatory standards. FDIC, OCC, NCUA, or NYDFS depending on charter and state.

And community banks face this collision under unusual pressure: a 36-hour notification rule, core vendor concentration, and the same threat actors that target the largest financial institutions.

How Coverage Gaps Show Up at Claim Time

Three real examples from policy reviews. Different banks, different carriers, same pattern.

Wire Fraud

A $400,000 wire goes out the door

A lending officer wires $400,000 against a spoofed email. The cyber policy excludes social engineering. The fidelity bond's voluntary parting exclusion applies. The funds transfer endorsement carries a 50% co-payment.

Recoverable: $200,000 on a $400,000 loss.
Regulatory Investigation

50,000 records, three proceedings, one gap

A breach exposes 50,000 customer records. Three proceedings follow within 60 days. The D&O policy excludes anything arising out of a cyber event. The cyber policy excludes D&O-style claims. Directors face personal liability.

In every institution I have reviewed, some version of this gap exists.
Security Warranty Rescission

Coverage rescinded after the breach

The cyber application asks whether MFA is enforced on all remote access. The bank answers yes. A core banking vendor's admin portal does not. The carrier rescinds coverage on warranty grounds.

A gap in your controls is a gap in your coverage.
