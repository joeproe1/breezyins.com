Risk Advisory for Community Banks

Nobody Has Read All Three Policies Together

Your bank carries cyber insurance, a fidelity bond, and a D&O policy. Most likely, each is from a different carrier, with different definitions and different exclusions.

When a cyber incident hits, these policies point fingers at each other. Each carrier writes exclusions assuming another policy covers it. For incidents like wire fraud, vendor breaches, or ransomware followed by a regulatory investigation, the result is gaps where no carrier pays.

What I Do

I read the actual policy language across all three policies, then map out how they interact against real incident scenarios your bank is likely to face. This is not a policy summary. It is a forensic review of how your insurance program responds when tested.

What You Get

Cyber Policy Review

Adequacy of sublimits, security warranties, incident response coverage, and regulatory proceeding definitions.

Fidelity Bond Review

Does your bond actually pay when an employee is tricked into wiring funds, or does the fine print exclude it?

D&O Review (Cyber Angle)

If regulators or shareholders come after your board post-breach, does your D&O respond or exclude cyber events?

Policy Interaction Map

Which policy responds, which denies, and where the seams are. Mapped against the incident scenarios that matter for your bank.

Recommendations

What to fix and how. Specific, actionable steps you can take to your broker or directly to your carriers.

Executive Summary with Risk Scorecard

Board-ready overview with uninsured exposure quantified in dollar terms.

Why This Matters for You and Your Board

Regulators increasingly expect boards to understand their bank's cyber risk posture. Whether your examiner is from the FDIC, OCC, or NCUA, the question is the same:

"How do you know your insurance is adequate?"

They expect a better answer than "Our broker said we are covered." This report gives you that answer, with the analysis behind it.

What I Need from You to Get Started

Three documents (full policies, not just the declarations pages):

  1. Your current cyber insurance policy
  2. Your fidelity bond / financial institution bond
  3. Your D&O policy

Plus some basic information about your bank: name, asset size, number of employees, and primary regulator.
