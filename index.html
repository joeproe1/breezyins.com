Three Ways MSP Insurance Fails

"Lost the enterprise deal"

Fortune 500 prospect requires proof of $5M cyber coverage. Your policy tops out at $2M.
Deal dies in procurement.

"Sued after a ransomware incident"

Client's network encrypted after your remote access was compromised. Your carrier says it's a cyber claim. Cyber carrier says it's a professional services claim.
You're stuck with legal bills.

"Broker sold you the wrong coverage"

You told your broker you do pen testing and incident response. They quoted 'IT consulting' and excluded security services.
You find out when you file a claim.

These coverage gaps hide in policy language and surface during contract reviews or claims. By then it is too late to fix them.

The Cost of Waiting

Enterprise clients now require $5M cyber limits, SOC 2 compliance, and indemnification clauses your current coverage will not back up. Every new deal you sign with misaligned insurance creates exposure you are personally liable for.

Meanwhile, carriers are tightening. Exclusions for systemic risk, infrastructure outages, and acts of war were added in the last 18 months. The claims that get denied most often are the ones where two policies both say the other should pay.

If you have not had your policies read against your contracts in the last 12 months, you are operating on assumptions.
