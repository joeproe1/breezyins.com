Three Situations That Expose Your Risk

Signing an Enterprise Contract

Your prospect's legal team wants proof of coverage you don't have

Enterprise contracts require specific limits, indemnification language, and Additional Insured status. Most MSP policies don't meet them.

Growing Your Business

You added pen testing last year. Your policy still says 'IT consulting'.

Your carrier covers the services you described on your application. If your business changed, that gap is yours.

Facing a Compliance Audit

SOC 2 says your controls work. Does your insurance?

If your policy doesn't match what you told the auditors, that documentation gap becomes your coverage gap.

These gaps hide in the policy language and only surface during contract reviews, compliance audits, or claims.
By then it is too late to fix them.

What You Walk Away With

A plain-English report that maps your actual business exposure. Not a renewal quote or sales pitch for a new policy.

  • Whether your MSA commitments match what your policies cover
  • Where your cyber and Tech E&O policies point fingers at each other and neither responds in a claim
  • Which services you deliver that your carrier does not know about or has excluded
