Three Situations That Expose Your Bank

Wire Fraud

Your cyber carrier says it's a bond claim. Your bond carrier says it's cyber.

An employee clicks a spoofed email. A wire transfer goes out. Your cyber policy excludes voluntary transfers. Your fidelity bond requires a specific trigger you can't prove. Nobody pays.

Board Liability

Regulators investigate. Your D&O won't cover it.

After a breach, regulators examine board oversight of cybersecurity. Your D&O excludes claims arising from a cyber event. Your cyber policy doesn't cover board defense costs. The gap falls on the institution.

Security Warranty

You had coverage. You thought.

Your cyber policy requires MFA on all remote access. Your core banking vendor doesn't support it. After a breach, the carrier rescinds coverage based on a warranty you didn't know you were making.

These gaps hide in policy language and surface during examinations, board reviews, or claims.
By then it is too late to fix them.
