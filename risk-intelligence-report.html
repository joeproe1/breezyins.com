Risk Intelligence Report

A fast, plain-English review of your insurance and client contracts, designed to show you where coverage will respond, where it will fail, and what to fix before you face a claim or lose a deal.

What the Report Does

The report flags the gaps that create surprises during enterprise onboarding and claims. You get specific policy-language findings and practical next steps, not generic risk scoring, for MSPs who already have insurance and want to know if it will actually cover what they do.

What you get

Coverage Gap Analysis

Line-by-line check of your insurance policies against the services you actually deliver: managed services, security assessments, pen testing, incident response, cloud migrations.

Contract Compliance Review

Review of up to 3 client agreements (MSAs/SOWs) to flag where limits, deductibles, cancellation notice, or required wording don't match.

Practical claim-readiness

Real-world scenarios showing what happens when you file a claim: ransomware incidents, client lawsuits, data breaches, and whether your policy will actually pay out.

Fixes you can act on

Clear recommendations on what to change, why it matters, and what to ask your broker to update.

Designed for MSPs with up to ~50 employees, who want clear answers without months of process or a large consulting bill.

How It Works

No long engagements, no complex onboarding. Four steps from first conversation to fixes in place.

1. Get in touch

Tell me about your MSP, the services you deliver, and your insurance concerns. A 15-minute call is usually enough to scope the review.

2. Send your policies

Share your current Tech E&O and Cyber policies, and your most important client contracts (MSAs) with me. I handle the rest.

3. Get the findings

A plain-English report with specific findings, exposure estimates, and recommended next steps. Includes a 30-minute walkthrough so you can ask questions and truly understand the findings.

4. Fix what matters

You can take the findings to your current broker, or I can help you negotiate policy changes and endorsements directly. Most fixes are straightforward once you know what to ask for.
